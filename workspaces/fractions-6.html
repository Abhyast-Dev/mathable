What is a Fraction?

A fraction represents a part of a whole or a part of a collection. The whole must be divided into equal parts for the fraction to be meaningful.

  • Numerator: the top number. It shows how many parts are selected.
  • Denominator: the bottom number. It shows the total number of equal parts.

Key Rules and Definitions

Proper Fraction

Numerator is smaller than denominator. Example: 3/5.

Improper Fraction

Numerator is greater than or equal to denominator. Example: 7/5.

Equivalent Fractions

Fractions that show the same value. Example: 1/2 = 2/4 = 4/8.

Simplest Form

Divide numerator and denominator by their HCF until no common factor remains.

Real-World Use

🍲 Cooking

Recipes use 1/2 cup, 3/4 spoon, and 2/3 bowl measurements. Fractions help scale recipes up or down.

🏏 Cricket

If a batter scores 3/4 of a team’s runs, fractions help compare contributions.

🛒 Shopping

A 25% discount means 1/4 off. Understanding fractions helps estimate prices quickly.

🍕 Sharing

Pizza, cake, chocolate bars, and fruit are divided fairly using fractions.
