Fact Sheet: Decimals

Decimals help us measure the “little differences” between whole numbers. They are useful for length, weight, money, time, and science.

Decimal Point: The point separates whole-number places from fractional places.
Place Value: 4.372 means 4 ones, 3 tenths, 7 hundredths, and 2 thousandths.
Fraction Connection: 0.1 = 1/10, 0.01 = 1/100, 0.001 = 1/1000.

Formula Hub

Tenths

1 tenth = 0.1 = 1/10

Example: 0.6 = 6/10

Hundredths

1 hundredth = 0.01 = 1/100

Example: 0.45 = 45/100

Thousandths

1 thousandth = 0.001 = 1/1000

Example: 0.125 = 125/1000

Real-World Use

Measurement: 2.5 cm is more precise than saying “about 2 cm”.
Money: ₹4.75 means 4 rupees and 75 paise.
Sports: Race timings like 10.42 seconds use decimals.
Science: Weight, temperature, and length often need decimal measurements.
