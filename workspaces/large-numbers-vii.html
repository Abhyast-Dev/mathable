What are Large Numbers?

Large numbers help us describe populations, heights, money, stadium capacity, city data, and everyday quantities that are too large to count one by one.

One lakh: \(1,00,000\), or 1 followed by 5 zeroes.
One crore: \(1,00,00,000\), or 1 followed by 7 zeroes. It is 100 lakhs.
Indian comma pattern: After the first three digits from the right, digits are grouped in pairs: 3-2-2-2...

Type a number to see its Indian and International comma grouping.

Rules Hub

One Lakh

\(1,00,000=100,000\)

It has 5 zeroes.

One Crore

\(1,00,00,000=10,000,000\)

It has 7 zeroes.

One Arab

\(1,00,00,00,000\)

It is 100 crores.

Indian Grouping

Use 3-2-2-2... grouping from the right.

Example: 9876501234 → 9,87,65,01,234.

International Grouping

Use 3-3-3... grouping from the right.

Example: 9876501234 → 9,876,501,234.

Quick Multipliers

\(\times 5 = \times10 \div 2\), \(\times25 = \times100 \div 4\), \(\times125 = \times1000 \div 8\).
