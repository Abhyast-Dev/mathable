How Large is Large?

This Smart Lab builds a feel for one lakh, one crore, population data, distances, large calculations, and approximation.

1 lakh = 1,00,000. It is one followed by 5 zeroes.
1 crore = 1,00,00,000. It is one followed by 7 zeroes. 100 lakhs make 1 crore.
Indian commas use a 3-2-2-2 pattern from right to left: 12,78,830.

Large Numbers in Daily Life

Population: City populations help us compare lakhs and crores.
Farming: One lakh varieties is easier to imagine when compared with days in a lifetime.
Stadiums: One lakh can feel big or small depending on the context.
Distance: Moon and Sun travel questions help students calculate with large products in stages.

Topic Map Used

  • A lakh varieties and large-number sense.
  • Reading and writing numbers in the Indian place value system.

Learning Outcomes

  • Read/write large numbers using Indian place value.
  • Explain lakh, crore, and their relationship to thousands.
  • Compose/decompose numbers using place-value buttons.
  • Estimate and round large numbers appropriately.
  • Use shortcuts for ×5, ×25, ×50, and ×125.
