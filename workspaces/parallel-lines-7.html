What are Parallel and Intersecting Lines?

On a plane surface, two lines can meet at a point, meet at right angles, or never meet however far they are extended.

Intersecting lines: A pair of lines that meet at one point. When two lines intersect, four angles are formed.
Parallel lines: Lines on the same plane that do not meet, however far they are extended.
Perpendicular lines: Intersecting lines that form right angles of 90°.

Rules Hub

Linear Pair

Adjacent angles on a straight line add up to 180°.

\(\angle a+\angle b=180^\circ\)

Vertically Opposite Angles

Opposite angles formed by two intersecting lines are equal.

\(\angle a=\angle c,\ \angle b=\angle d\)

Perpendicular Lines

Two intersecting lines are perpendicular if they form right angles.

Each angle is \(90^\circ\).

Corresponding Angles

When corresponding angles formed by a transversal are equal, the two lines are parallel.

Alternate Angles

Alternate angles formed by a transversal cutting parallel lines are equal.

Transversal

A line that intersects two different lines. It forms eight angles.

Teacher Tools

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify intersecting, perpendicular, and parallel lines on a plane.
  • Use linear pairs to calculate missing angles.
  • Recognise vertically opposite angles and justify why they are equal.
  • Use corresponding angles to test whether two lines are parallel.
  • Use alternate angles formed by a transversal across parallel lines.
  • Connect classroom paper-folding activities to geometric reasoning.

Exit Ticket Prompts

  • If one angle is 120° when two lines intersect, find the other three angles.
  • Why do perpendicular lines create four 90° angles?
  • How can corresponding angles help us decide if two lines are parallel?
